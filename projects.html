ENGS 31 (Digital Electronics): Combinational Lock

For our final project, my parter and I created a combination lock with LED indicators and an autolock-out mode. Our project required a Basys3 FPGA board, Pmod-Keypad, Pmod-breadboard, 2 LEDs, and 2 resistors. We used VHDL to program the Basys3 board.

Our combinational lock compares a user's 4-digit keypad input to a hardwired solution. The Basys3 board is connected to the breadboard with 2 output LED indicators that are wired to turn on and off depending on the solution. If the user makes three consecutive failed passcode attempts, the autolock feature engages and the lock cannot be operated until ten seconds have passed (in a real world application this would be longer). When the lock is in autolock mode, the characters ‘dEAd’ on the seven segment display. This function is similar to that found on many popular smartphones and prevents brute force entry to the device through repeated password attempts.
